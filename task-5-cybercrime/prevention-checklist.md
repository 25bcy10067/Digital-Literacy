**Stay Safe Online: Checklist for College Students in India**  
   
Here is a useful checklist to help college students in India protect themselves online, focusing on financial and UPI safety.   
   
1. **Use Official UPI Apps Only**  
   Download UPI apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or your bank’s app only from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid third-party links or websites to stay safe from fake or harmful apps.   
   
2. **Never Share Your UPI PIN or OTP**  
   Your UPI PIN and OTP are private. Banks, NPCI, or app representatives will never ask for them. If someone asks for these, it is a scam.  
   
3. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Financial Transactions**  
   Public networks are not secure. Always use your mobile data or a trusted, password-protected Wi-Fi network when making UPI payments or accessing bank accounts.   
   
4. **Verify Recipient Details Before Sending Money**   
   Double-check the UPI ID, name, and account number before completing a transaction. A small mistake can result in irreversible fund transfers.   
   
5. **Set Daily UPI Transaction Limits**  
   Customize your daily transaction limit in your UPI app according to your needs. This reduces potential losses in case of unauthorized access.   
   
6. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**  
   Use biometric locks (fingerprint, face ID) and strong passwords for your phone and UPI apps. This provides extra security in case your device is lost or stolen.   
   
7. **Don’t Click on Suspicious Links or Pop-ups**  
   Scammers often send fake payment links through SMS or WhatsApp. Never click on unsolicited links, even if they seem to come from known contacts or banks.   
   
8. **Monitor Transactions Regularly**  
   Check your UPI and bank transaction history every week. Report any unauthorized activity right away.  
   
**In Case of Fraud: Report Immediately**  
- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal**: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)    
- **Helpline**: Dial  **1930** (24/7) for reporting financial fraud    
File a complaint online or call within 2 hours for the best chance of recovering your funds.
