**Social Media Checklist**  
   **✅ Do's for Responsible Use**  
    **Define your goals and audience**     **:** Clearly identify who you want to reach (e.g., peers, faculty, future employers) and what you want to accomplish, such as increasing awareness of events or networking.  
**Audit privacy settings regularly**     **:** Deep dive into the privacy settings of every account to control visibility, turn off location-sharing data, and restrict which apps can access your personal information.  
    
**Verify before sharing personal info**     **:** If you receive unsolicited requests for personal details—even from acquaintances—ignore them or verify the identity through a different, secure method like a phone call or in-person conversation.  
   
**Review content before posting**     **:** Take time to inspect photos and videos on a larger screen to ensure no sensitive information (like passwords, names, or grades) is visible in the background or file metadata.  
    
**Engage with positive trends:** Browse platforms for inspiration, engage with relevant content, and reshare material that builds brand loyalty or contributes to meaningful industry conversations.  
   
   
**❌ Don'ts for Responsible Use**  
   
    **Never share identifiable student or personal data:** Avoid posting faces, names, grades, or any part of an educational record without explicit consent, and never share details like addresses, birth dates, or Social Security numbers.  
   
  Create digital files using naming conventions that do not include first or last names to prevent accidental disclosure of identity when sharing files.  
   
    
 **Avoid over-tagging or spamming**: Refrain from adding hashtags to every word or over-tagging posts, as this can reduce engagement and appear unprofessional; for example, Facebook users engage more with a single hashtag.  
   
    
 **Do not instantly share photos or videos:** Resist the urge to post immediately after taking a picture, as this increases the risk of accidentally revealing sensitive information like classroom passwords or student profiles.  
   
    
 **Don't ignore algorithm changes or post inconsistently:** Avoid posting at random times or sticking to a single strategy forever, as algorithms frequently change and inconsistent posting can hurt your visibility and engagement.
